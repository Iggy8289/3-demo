Strategic Document Analysis

Using AI for competitive positioning in complex RFPs

The Situation

Project: Contra Costa County Fleet EVSE (Electric Vehicle Service Equipment)

  • 14 sites, 178 charging ports for county fleet vehicles
  • 11 firms responded to the RFQ
  • Two-phase selection: RFQ shortlists 3-4 firms ‚Üí RFP for final selection

The Challenge: This was a "no-go" situation by normal criteria.

Why normally a no-go: Charging-only projects aren't OPTERRA's sweet spot. We excel at integration and complexity (EV + BESS + Solar + Building Systems). Competitors with deeper charging-only portfolios would have natural advantages.

Why we pursued anyway: Strategic customer relationship. Contra Costa County represents long-term partnership potential beyond this single project.

The Question: How do we position competitively when we're NOT the obvious favorite?

The AI-Assisted Approach

Step 1: RFP Analysis & Criteria Extraction

What I did: Uploaded the RFQ document to Claude

Initial prompt: "Analyze this RFQ and identify: (1) all evaluation criteria, (2) relative weighting or importance, (3) what evaluators are really looking for beyond stated requirements."

What AI provided:
  • 8 evaluation criteria extracted and ranked by importance
  • Key differentiators identified (prior County experience, CEC grant expertise, multi-site coordination)
  • Potential gaps highlighted (charging-only vs integrated approach positioning)

Step 2: Competitive Assessment

What I did: Shared OPTERRA's capabilities and project history

Follow-up prompt: "Given OPTERRA's profile, assess our competitive position against these criteria. What are our strengths? Where are we vulnerable? How should we position?"

What AI identified:
  • Major strengths: Prior Contra Costa County relationship, Oakland headquarters (truly local), 300+ PG&E interconnections, comprehensive ESCO approach
  • Vulnerabilities: Less charging-specific experience shown, could appear more expensive than equipment-only competitors
  • Positioning strategy: Lead with County relationship and integration capabilities, frame comprehensiveness as risk mitigation rather than cost increase

Step 3: Initial Probability Assessment

What I asked:

Critical question: "Based on this RFQ, OPTERRA's profile, and typical competitive field, assess our likelihood of making top 3-4 shortlist. Be honest about gaps."

AI's initial assessment:

Current Score: 72-78/100

Assessment: "Borderline. Current draft needs strengthening to confidently make top 3-4."

Risk: "Could miss shortlist if competitors have stronger county fleet EVSE portfolios"

Step 4: Implementing Improvements

What I asked:

Action-oriented: "What specific changes would improve our score? Prioritize by impact."

AI's recommendations (implemented):
  • Complete all personnel biographies (was incomplete)
  • Add 1-2 county fleet yard EVSE examples to portfolio
  • Strengthen DCFC experience narrative
  • Add office locations detail (missing section)
  • Adjust timeline assumptions to realistic values

Step 5: Reassessment After Improvements

AI's Updated Scoring Analysis: After implementing recommendations, score improved from 72-78 to 89-96 across all eight evaluation criteria. AI assessment: "With recommended improvements, you should comfortably exceed the 82+ threshold for top 3 selection."

Updated Score: 89-96/100

Likelihood of Shortlist: 90-95%

Assessment: "Top 3-4 respondents. Strong competitive position."

The Outcome

Actual Result: #1 out of 11 Respondents

OPTERRA ranked first in the RFQ phase, advancing to RFP negotiations.

What this validated:

  • AI's probability assessment was accurate (predicted 90-95% shortlist likelihood)
  • The positioning strategy worked (won despite being outside typical sweet spot)
  • Iterative refinement improved outcomes (72-78 ‚Üí 87 score correlation with #1 ranking)
  • The methodology is replicable for future RFPs

What You Can Learn From This

üéØ This Isn't About AI "Magic"

AI didn't write the response or make strategic decisions. It helped me think systematically about positioning by:

  • Extracting criteria I might have missed
  • Identifying gaps before submission
  • Quantifying competitive position objectively
  • Suggesting improvements I could evaluate

I still made all strategic decisions. AI was a thought partner, not a decision-maker.

üìö The Skill is Strategic Questioning

Success came from knowing what questions to ask:

  • "What are evaluators really looking for?" (beyond stated criteria)
  • "What's our competitive position honestly?" (objective assessment)
  • "What's our probability?" (quantifying uncertainty)
  • "What would improve our position?" (action-oriented)
  • "Now what's our probability?" (validation loop)

You don't need to be technical. You need to be strategic about what information you need.

üîÑ Iterative Refinement Works

This wasn't one prompt ‚Üí perfect answer. It was a conversation:

  1. Upload document ‚Üí Get initial analysis
  2. Share context ‚Üí Get competitive assessment
  3. Ask for probability ‚Üí Get honest evaluation (72-78)
  4. Ask what improves ‚Üí Get specific recommendations
  5. Implement changes ‚Üí Validate new probability (90-95)
  6. Submit ‚Üí Achieve #1 ranking

The methodology matters more than any single output.

Your First Step (If This Interests You)

Try This Tomorrow:

  1. Find a complex document you need to understand (RFP, technical report, contract, strategy document)
  2. Upload it to Claude (Claude.ai - free tier works fine for this)
  3. Ask a strategic question: "What are the three most important things in this document that I should focus on?"
  4. Follow up based on response: "Given [your context], what's my biggest risk here?"
  5. Keep iterating until you have clarity

That's it. You don't need coding skills. You don't need technical expertise. You need curiosity and good questions.

Time investment: 30 minutes experimenting. If it helps, you'll know immediately.
