Engineering Acceleration

Using AI to remove preliminary assessment bottlenecks

The Situation

Project: School District Electric Bus Fleet Electrification

  • Client considering transition to electric school buses
  • Needed preliminary BESS (Battery Energy Storage System) sizing
  • Required for feasibility assessment and budget planning
  • Multiple scenarios to explore (different peak shaving targets)

The Challenge: Engineering team backlogged with active projects.

Typical timeline for preliminary BESS assessment: 3-5 days

  • Day 1: Gather fleet data, understand operational patterns
  • Day 2-3: Model load profiles, calculate energy requirements
  • Day 3-4: Size BESS (power and energy ratings)
  • Day 4-5: Economic analysis, create client presentation

The problem: Client needs quick feasibility response to decide whether to pursue full RFP. Can't wait a week for preliminary numbers.

The risk: If we respond slowly, client moves on to competitors who can provide faster preliminary guidance.

The Question: Could AI help accelerate preliminary technical analysis without sacrificing quality?

  • Calculate BESS power requirements (kW)
  • Show economic payback estimates
  • Visualize load profile with/without BESS
  • Update everything in real-time as user adjusts targets

How the calculations work:

Input: Electric bus fleet load profile (15-minute intervals over 24 hours)

For each peak shaving target (e.g., 50 kW):

  1. Calculate excess power: For each 15-min interval, find power above target
    Excess = max(0, Original_Demand - Target_Peak)
  2. Calculate excess energy: Energy that BESS must store
    Excess_Energy = Excess_Power √ó 0.25 hours
  3. Sum total energy: How much storage capacity needed
    Required_BESS_Energy = Œ£(Excess_Energy)
  4. Find maximum power: BESS power rating needed
    Required_BESS_Power = max(Excess_Power)
  5. Estimate costs: BESS cost and annual savings
    Simple_Payback = BESS_Cost / Annual_Savings

Why this methodology works:

  • Based on established power systems engineering principles
  • Uses standard 15-minute interval data (industry norm)
  • Conservative assumptions (oversizes slightly for safety)
  • Appropriate for preliminary feasibility, not final design

What You Can Learn From This

üéØ AI Excels at "Established Methodology" Problems

This worked because BESS sizing follows known engineering principles:

  • The physics is established (energy storage = excess power √ó time)
  • The methodology is documented (15-minute intervals, peak shaving calculations)
  • The validation is straightforward (engineering team can verify)
  • The complexity is computational, not conceptual

Look for similar patterns in your work: Where do you apply established methodology repetitively? That's automatable.

This is preliminary assessment, NOT final design:

  • Appropriate for: Feasibility studies, budget planning, go/no-go decisions
  • NOT appropriate for: Final specifications, construction documents, utility approval
  • Always validated by: Engineering team review before client delivery
  • Client understanding: Clear labeling as "preliminary" assessment

The rule: AI accelerates preliminary work. Engineers validate and complete detailed design.
